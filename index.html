Festivals of India

India is a place of diverse cultures and religions, celebrated through a vibrant array of festivals throughout the year. These festivals are a glimpse into the rich traditions and customs of the country.
These are all the festivals I celebrate with
my family



Diwali
Also known as the "festival of lights," Diwali is a major festival celebrated with the lighting of
diyas (oil lamps), fireworks, and the exchange
of sweets. It typically falls in October or November. During Diwali, we make rangolis outside our doors and light diyas that we decorate. When I go to Delhi for the holidays, all my cousins come to our house at night, and we eat and listento music under the fairy lights in the garden.



Holi
Known as the "festival of colors," Holi is celebrated by throwing colored powders and water on each other, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. It's a joyous occasion marking the arrival of spring. When we spend Holi in Delhi, we bring out inflatable pools and pichkaris. All my cousins come to celebrate.
We love pranking our parents, so we set traps with water balloons and powder in the garden.



Raksha Bandhan
Raksha Bandhan is a festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters, where sisters tie a rakhi (sacred thread) on their brothers' wrists In return, brothers give gifts and promise to protect their sisters. In Delhi, I have many cousin brothers and many sisters. We all queue up to tie rakhis. At the end, our brother's wrists have at least 12-14 rakhis!



Ganesh Chaturthi
This 10-day festival is dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the god of wisdom and prosperity. Elaborate idols of Ganesha are installed, and prayers are offered, followed by a procession and immersion of the idols in water. On this
day, we go from house to house where the idols are installed. We eat a lot of sweets and enjoy.



Navratri
In Navratri, we spend 30 minutes everyday in my Dadi's room doing pooja. My dadi or my Kaka lead the shlokas, and my cousin sister
and I fight over who gets to ring the bell! We
go down in the building compound for dandiya with all the other families. There are so many lights and so much food.



Janamashtami
Janamashtami is my favourite Festival. Every year, my mom and I wake up early to set up
tiny models of Krishnaji's story. We read the stories about him to my cousin sister. We've been reading these books since I was four. We set up the dioramas around the house and my dadi prepares food with makkhan. Every meal has a dish with a makkhan component!



Makar Sankranti
My Kaka takes us to the terrace in the evening to fly kites he bought before. We joke about the kites we see that cut each other off, like once, my Kaka's kite collided with my and they both fell! I don't go up to fly kites anymore, though.



Eid
Eid-al-fitr is huge celebration celebrated by many people all aorund the world. It is celebrated by gathering with loved ones,
sharing festive sweets and meals, giving gifts, and enoying a spirit of joy.



Christmas
Christmas is also known as the festival of joy, and is celebrated in many, many countries as time time of togetherness and giving. It's marked by decorating homes with trees and lights, exchanging presents, prepping festive meals and spending time with loved ones. Some festive colours are red, green, white and gold.

Note: These are only few of the many festivals of India.
